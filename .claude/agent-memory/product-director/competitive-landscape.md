## Category Definitions

**Inventory Specialist** — unit is a SKU+quantity. Buyer is ops/purchasing. Items are interchangeable.
Tools: Cin7, inFlow, Fishbowl, Zoho Inventory.

**Asset Tracker** — unit is a unique serialized physical object. Buyer is IT/facilities/warehouse ops.
Tools: Sortly, Asset Panda, Asset Tiger.

**Loon's category:** Asset Tracker with inventory workflow layered on top. Unique serialized assets (never interchangeable SKUs) + full operational lifecycle (arrivals → holds → transfers → departures). No clean home in either category — this is the product opportunity.

## Competitor Profiles

**Cin7** — Inventory Specialist. 10–200 employees, product/wholesale/3PL. SKU+qty, weak serialization. ~$349–999+/mo. Killer: multichannel order mgmt. Weakness: overwhelming complexity, serialization bolted on. Relevance: workflow depth inspiration; not a direct competitor (too heavy for Loon ICP).

**Sortly** — Asset Tracker. 1–50 employees, retail/field ops. SKU+qty, limited serial support. ~$29–149/mo. Killer: beautiful mobile app, photo documentation. Weakness: no sales workflow, no receiving, no warehouse ops — stops at "where is it?" Relevance: CLOSEST DIRECT COMPETITOR on UX and customer size. Study their onboarding and mobile UX.

**Asset Panda** — Asset Tracker. 50–500 employees, IT/facilities. Serialized individual assets. ~$1,500+/yr. Killer: custom fields + check-in/check-out workflows. Weakness: clunky UI, enterprise-feel, no warehouse movement workflow. Relevance: closest use-case match; beatable on UX and warehouse workflow. Study their data model; avoid their UX choices.

**Asset Tiger** — Asset Tracker. 5–100 employees, IT/facilities. Serialized individual assets. Free–~$350/yr. Killer: price — full features nearly free. Weakness: dated UI, limited integrations, feels like abandonware. Relevance: low-end flank; not the product to beat.

## Strategic Positioning

- **Sortly** = most dangerous near-term competitor. Same SMB size, same beautiful-app positioning. Tops out at "locate your stuff" — no arrivals, departures, sales, warehouse transfers. That's Loon's wedge.
- **Asset Panda** = use-case match but product playbook to AVOID. Got serialized-asset model right but built it enterprise-heavy. Reviews complain about UI complexity and onboarding friction.
- **Directive:** Study Sortly's onboarding and mobile UX. Study Asset Panda's data model. Build neither product.

**Why:** Validated during competitive landscape analysis session.
**How to apply:** Use Sortly as the UX benchmark. Use Asset Panda's data model as the functional benchmark. Any feature comparison should reference these two first.
